## The model in one line
Keep the familiar aerobic/threshold machinery running all winter; then change
only a small portion of the week so it resembles racing — never a wholesale
program switch.

## Verified structure
- **Base = the same week for months.** Jakob: same Mon-Sun program ~Oct-Apr,
  largely unchanged for ~15 years. Almgren: a fixed structure held for ~6
  weeks, then ~2 weeks of race preparation.
- Progression in base comes from **volume, durability and pace-at-same-effort**
  — not from new workouts. Jakob's 400s progressed 16x400 -> 20x400 -> 25x400
  over years, at the same recoveries.
- **Specific phase is short**: Jakob ~3-4 weeks of focused work (plus early
  races as specificity); Almgren ~2 weeks of "gradually phasing in race
  speed" — his words: getting *accustomed to speed*, not making every workout
  harder.
- **Final big session 10-12 days out** (Almgren), then freshness — no
  fitness-proving sessions in the last days. Almgren reports ~0-2 maximal
  workouts per *year*; nearly everything stays controlled.

## Verified sessions
- Jakob AM threshold: **4x6min building to threshold**, often treadmill,
  multiple mornings/week, evaluated while carrying weeks of fatigue.
- Jakob weekly year-round: **25x400 w/ 30s** at "10K+ pace" early season,
  drifting toward 5K pace as racing nears. Reach target pace by rep ~4-6,
  then **hold stable** — no accelerating; the last 4-5 reps get muscularly
  hard by themselves from accumulated fatigue.
- Almgren 10K-specific (end of a ~170 km week): **3x3km w/ 3min**, progressive
  8:00 / 7:52 / 7:43 (2:40 -> 2:34/km).
- Almgren final pre-race check: **10x1km w/ 1min**, avg 2:34/km.
- Almgren 5K-specific: **10x600 + 10x200** (splits unpublished).
- Almgren week shape: AM 6-min reps; PM 1km reps or 25x400; two controlled
  double-threshold days/week.

## Half marathon conversion (Almgren, verified)
- **The week stays whole.** Tue/Thu double threshold unchanged, Sunday long
  unchanged, mileage unchanged — his threshold speeds are already near HM
  pace, so the base IS most of the preparation.
- **One swap: Saturday hills -> a long progressive HM-specific session.**
- Verified session: **3x5km progressive on rolling roads: 14:43 / 14:25 /
  14:12** (2:57 -> 2:50/km); recovery not published. He raced 58:41
  (2:47/km) three weeks later — so the reps sat ~10 / ~6 / ~3.5 s/km off
  race pace: controlled threshold -> strong threshold -> near-HM effort,
  NOT 3 x race simulation. In percent-of-10k terms: ~91 -> 95%.
- His reasoning: after a track season he was anaerobically strong; the
  missing piece was running fast for a long time — so longer reps and more
  km near HM intensity, not faster short work.
- Dedicated conversion: **~3 weeks** after a full 5k/10k season. Final 10-14
  days: reduce fatigue, keep threshold rhythm + some HM-pace exposure.
- Most of the session volume stays controlled; only the final portion
  (~ the last third) approaches race intensity.
- Kejelcha (57:30 WR) and Korir (58:50): no credibly published HM training
  blocks exist as of 2026-07-19 — checked, nothing stored.

## Application rules
1. Base: repeat the identical week; don't rotate workouts for variety.
2. **Race-specific sessions are distance-based at the real rep distances**
   (25x400m, 3x3km, 10x1km) — in the specific phase, specificity beats
   physiological control: you go for it. Time-based conversion applies to
   base threshold work only (Ben's rule, 2026-07-19).
3. ~5-6 weeks out: keep the whole week, move ONE session (the 400s) toward
   race pace over ~2 weeks — familiarisation, not extra strain.
4. ~3 weeks out: convert one threshold session to a race-pace stepping stone
   (3x3km progressive to ~race effort), roughly every 10-14 days.
5. Final big session 10-12 days before the race (10x1km @ race pace w/ 1min);
   after it, only controlled sub-threshold + easy running.
6. Race week: ~half volume, 2-3 short race-pace touches early, easy + strides
   the last days.
7. The sub-threshold AM machinery (5-6min reps @ 91-93%) never leaves the
   week — it's the platform everything else stands on.
8. For 5k prep, Jakob's only verified race-specific session IS the 25x400
   drifting to 5K pace — no new workout; his other 5k sharpening comes from
   racing itself (May-June races before the summer peak).
9. Specific-phase length scales with the event's distance from the base
   work: 5k/10k need ~5 weeks of gradual speed conversion; the half needs
   only ~3 weeks (threshold pace is already near HM pace — just swap
   Saturday hills for the long progressive session).
10. If the race is close, keep the END of the sequence and drop the front:
   the final big session 10-12 days out and the race-week taper matter
   most; the early familiarisation weeks are the expendable part. A short
   block sharpens what's there — it never replaces missing base.
